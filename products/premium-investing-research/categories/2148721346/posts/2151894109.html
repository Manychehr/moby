Why These Are Our Top 2 Healthcare Stocks of 2021

Published on September 7, 2021

While we don't write about healthcare stocks often, they are extremely important to watch out for. The healthcare sector in the United States alone represents over 3 trillion dollars and makes up over 17% of the United States GDP! And this number is projected to grow up to 25% by 2030!

What this means is that healthcare is projected to become the biggest market in the United States and therefore the opportunity to invest in its future cannot be ignored.

Health Care List Overview:

While the stocks below are not household names like Apple or Tesla, these companies either are massive or have long term potential for serious growth.

And if you're not sold on owning healthcare stocks, diversification is a key sticking point as to why they should be included in a portfolio. As we've preached here at Moby before, diversifying your portfolio is very important! Diversification is important should any sell-off's to certain sectors occur, you can "hedge" yourself from these sector specific sell-offs. Conversely if a sector were to appreciate, and you were diversified, you may now be able to participate in a rally that you previously were unable to participate in!

 

Top 2 Healthcare Stocks:

So with that context, the list below contains 2 of our favorite stocks to invest in within the healthcare sector! Please let us know if you have any questions! Please note healthcare also consists of several verticals such as but not limited to: Pharmaceuticals, Biotech, Equipment, Tech, etc.

1) Schrodinger Inc (SDGR): Schrodinger is an American life sciences and materials science company founded in 1990 that develops software for computational chemistry and has a pipeline of collaborative and internal drug discovery programs.

We are initiating an overweight rating on SDGR for two main reasons. This is because:

  1. Their current stock price is extremely attractive given the stock is down 20% this year. Even with sales growing, the market cap has contracted leaving the stock undervalued. With what should be strong second half growth, we see the stock reversing course soon.
  2. The reason shares have been trending downwards was largely due to the guidance the company issued back in Q1 of this year. Since then investors panic sold and the stock price dropped more than it should have. Given how seasonal their contracts are, we believe sales should spike in Q3 & Q4 leading to large outperformance vs what people are expecting.

 

 

2) Veeva Systems (VEEV): Veeva Systems Inc. is an American cloud-computing company focused on pharmaceutical and life sciences industry applications. It works with software as a service (SaaS) in the global life-science industry.

We'v written about VEEV before as one of our top healthcare stocks but our new analysis shows the following. VEEV should be a strong name for healthcare investors over the next few years.

  • Strong Revenue Growth: During their most recent earnings report we see that their revenue clocked in at $456M and was well ahead of what people thought ($451M). When looking more closely at what caused the outperformance, we see that their subscription revenue drove most of the growth. Knowing that subscription revenue has high margins and is scalable, we're extremely excited to see this driving the outperformance. We think the market missed this and therefore are getting in now.

 

 

BONUS CONTENT:

We also like the stock Biogen (BIIB) but they've gone through some strong volatility this year. The reason for the volatility is largely due to their drug helping fight Alzheimers. The US FDA first approved Biogen's treatment on June 7, 2021 for early stage Alzheimer's disease but since has gone through strong backlash. The reason this was so monumental was because it was the first new therapy approved for the disorder in nearly two decades - marking some serious success against the growing and terrible diseases. However, since the ruling, there has been serious backlash in the medical community and the results are still murky.

Therefore, we are waiting further before initiating a rating but do note the other strong potential drugs in their pipeline.

If you are a strong risk taking investor, this could have some nice upside but also comes with strong downside risk. Proceed with caution. We do not have the appetite for the associated risk at this time.
