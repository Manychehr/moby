PayPal: Does An Acquisition of Pinterest Make Sense?

Published on October 21, 2021

You may have seen the rumors that PayPal was looking to acquire Pinterest. If you haven't, then we're happy to break the news! 

But in any case, many investors are left wondering, PayPal and Pinterest, why? How do those two companies make sense together?

Add in the fact that the price tag for Pinterest's would likely be over $39 billion, and many people are left puzzled.

That's where we come in. We did all the hard and boring work for you. 

With PayPal recently hitting our price target we thought it was perfect timing to get into the details on the acquisition, whether or not it makes sense and then ultimately answer the most important question: How will this affect PayPal's stock price?

 

PayPal Overview:

While a lot of this deal is speculation to date, talks so far have indicated that this could announced by early next month! Therefore our analysts scrambled quickly to put together our best thinking on this subject fast.

The high level summary is that we actually think there is a ton of overlap between the two tech giants. While that may come as a surprise there are a few key areas that are signaling the potential synergies. They are:

  • By combining with Pinterest (PINS), PayPal (PYPL) should be able to deepen their relationships with existing and new merchants. The reason they should be able to do this is because they are not on the PINS platform today. By leveraging PINS platform, PayPal should be able to work with a whole host of new vendors in addition to satisfying the needs of a lot of their existing clients. While this integration and rollout would not happen overnight, this alone could pay back the purchase price quite quickly. This is because PINS makes over $1B in revenue per year and we estimate 75%+ overlap with existing PINS users. If PayPal can eventually continue to steal wallet share from the existing payment methods on PINS, they should be able to leverage significant revenue expansion at high margins!
  • By leveraging the PINS platform, PayPal can drastically increase their BNPL (buy now pay later) offering. In case you haven't been paying attention, BNPL is blowing up like crazy! Recently we wrote up Affirm (see: here) and their growth has been out of control (the stock is up 60% this year). PayPal clearly sees the writing on the wall, and by using PINS they can drastically increase their revenue from this channel. With massive growth in BNPL, investors will give this revenue stream a higher valuation, thus pushing their stock price higher for the same revenue growth. When you also factor in the size of PINS platform and scale of social commerce, we think PayPal sees this as a launching point to get involved across other social giants.

 

Deal Valuation:

While at first glance the overlap doesn't seem high, once you peel back the layers, the synergies become much more clear. While PINS is having growth issues of their own, the purchase price of the stock and the synergies alone could make up for any mishaps. While it is too early to model out the future cash flows of the deal, we think this has the possibility to revolutionize PayPal's business.

As a stock we've always liked, we view this event positively and thus push up our price target!
