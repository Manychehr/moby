Robinhood | Should you Buy or Sell?

Published on October 5, 2021

We'd be hard pressed to believe that anyone reading this right now doesn't know about Robinhood. Whether you like the company or not is up to you, but what cannot be denied is that they fundamentally changed the way how retail investors access and feel about the markets. A large portion of their success was due to a combination of business model and design. And now as one of the most controversial IPO's this year, many investors are left wondering, "Should I be investing in their stock?" If you're one of these investors or are curious about how we project the stock to perform - read on to learn more!

 
 

Robinhood Overview:

In order to review the stock, we need to discuss their differentiators and whether or not this is sustainable. From there we can review the financials and give our projections on where they're headed. So with that context, let's break down why they're even successful in the first place:

  • From Robinhood's inception till now, they did something that no one else was doing. They made finance fun. They did this by taking the best practices in design from other successful tech products and combining it together into a brokerage app. This is one of two main reasons that the app caught on as quickly as it did. Brokerage apps, were nothing new when they came to market, but the design for them was a huge X factor. They removed every friction point, made the design clean and gamified it.
  • The next thing they did was give access to the markets in a way no company had ever done before and this was done via PFOF (payment for order flow). PFOF is how Robinhood makes 77% of their revenue. This is how Robinhood allows you to trade for "free"! PFOF is when Robinhood sells your order flow to market makers. The reason market makers are interested in your order flow is because they have the ability to make money off of it! This often leads to worse exection for retail investors and comes as an intrinsic cost. What that means is that for slightly higher cost basis's in every stock a retail trader buys (and lower for sells), they can trade for free while the market makers, make money. For most retail traders, this doesn't affect their profit/losses too much but for market makers, at scale, this can add up to huge profits very quickly.

These two pillars have literally been responsible for most of the success that Robinhood has had to date. And unless PFOF gets banned (the SEC is talking about banning PFOF but we don't think this will happen any time soon but it will just become more regulated. And in any case none of this will happen overnight - it'll take a lot of time), it is likely what will keep them successful for the next decade. Pair that with new ways to democratize the markets and Robinhood's popularity continues to grow every day. Even in the face of intense scrutiny, Robinhood is growing fast! 

 

Revenue Stream:

So now that you understand how they make money and what makes differentiates them, let's talk about the projections of their business. The graph below shows the breakdown of the revenue they're receiving as part of PFOF.

 

What is so interesting about this, is how equities revenue is declining YoY while crypto and options are rising fast in popularity. This quarter last year crypto was pulling in nothing relative to their other parts of their business and now it is the leading revenue stream for Robinhood. This will likely fluctuate in the quarters to come but overall we view this as a strong signal for the company.

If Robinhood has such a strong diversification in their product suite, they'll be able to capture and maintain more users across a wider demographic.

 

Market Concentration:

The next interesting point to watch is how Robinhood is making this revenue. This quarter, Robinhood's largest market maker switched from Citadel (36% of market making last year) to Tai Mo Shan Limited (29% this year with Citadel at 14%). For those of you who have never heard of them, they are a Cayman Islands Company with effectively 0 public information.

This is an extremely interesting point to take notice of. Tai Mo Shan is a part of Jump Trading, which is based in Chicago. While we find it extremely difficult to believe that Robinhood would knowingly expose itself to another SEC scandal/fine first glance at this is extremely questionable. Outsourcing their order flow to a country where financial laws are extremely limited, is never a good sign. We note this as an extremely high risk factor for the company in any future legislation. However, in the short term, from what we uncovered, this likely comes with higher margins and therefore strong profitability.

 

How To Play It: 

So with all of this information, you may be left wondering, "so should I buy or sell?". The answer is a bit convoluted based on the factors above. But when comparing their revenue growth against competitors, we see them trading at a severe discount to Coinbase (who with this new revenue due to crypto) is a good comparable for the company. At a market cap of $36B vs Coinbase's $53B, we believe Robinhood presents a good buying opportunity. When looking at their revenue growth as well, we see that their valuation is actually lower than what we'd expect (trading at 18x forward looking revenue).

We think Coinbase's business is less defendable over the long term and Robinhood should be in a good position to continue to capture market share across most major retail investing opportunities. But as we noted above this pick comes with a fair share of risk. Because of the legal ramifications, paired with widening operating losses, Robinhood is subject to swings in either direction. Therefore this pick is very high risk but also very high reward.
