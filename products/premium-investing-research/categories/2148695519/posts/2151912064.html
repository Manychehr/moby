The Company Redefining How We Shop Online

Published on September 9, 2021

Have you ever heard of Affirm Holdings? They are the technology that sits on top of a lot of e-commerce stores that allow you to buy now and pay later (known as BNPL). If you still aren't familiar with that term, the essentially help you seamlessly break large payments into smaller ones.

Don't want to spend $1,500 on a Peloton? Affirm lets you break it down into smaller payments spread out over the course of a year or two. Regardless of whether you have or haven't heard of them, Affirm is growing rapidly. And a brand new partnership with one of the largest companies in the world, has us rethinking the merit of their expensive stock price. Let's break down whether or not we like the stock and what our price target is below!

 

Affirm 🤝 Amazon Partnership:

The massive news that was recently announced was that Affirm is becoming a payment method on Amazon. Still in the testing phase, the rollout should happen in a couple of months. This has massive implications for both parties as it allows consumers to start financing many of their purchases that they want to spread out over time. With over 150 million US based prime customers, Affirm will immediately gain access to their largest new offering, ever. So, how rapidly should this grow their business?

  • When looking at Affirm's current business we see that Affirm is projected to work with ~11 million active customers by the end of 2023.  The new partnership with Amazon is expected to bring that number closer to ~18 million active users.
That means Affirm can now grow their business 63% larger!

One single partnership has the ability to completely transform their trajectory over the next few years and we're truly excited to tell you how that will affect their business and stock price. While the market immediately realized this too (as the stock jumped up almost 50%), we still think there is more room to run for Affirm.

 

Amazon's Upside:

While this will positively affect Amazon's business too, the bigger win is for Affirm because of how small they are relative to Amazon. We forecast that this can increase Amazon's annual sales by up to $300M, but for a business doing over $200B in revenue per year, $300M isn't moving the needle too much.

 

Affirm's Upside:

The upside however for Affirm is clearly massive. While the partnership will directly expand their user base, there are two major points of growth that others are missing. They are:

  • As a B2B2C company (business to business to consumer - they sell to businesses but engage directly with consumers), brand development and interactivity can be a struggle. Currently Affirm interacts with users ~2x a year. But now Affirm has the ability to drastically grow their brand presence and how often they interact with consumers by partnering with Amazon. The reason this is so important is because Affirm works with many other major retailers too. By building up more brand value, recognition, and interactivity with consumers, the likelihood they interact with them on other sites goes up significantly as well. So this partnership has the ability to not only drive more users with Amazon directly but it also unlocks synergies across their other partners too!
  • Right now most of Affirm's business is done in the United States. While the partnership with Amazon will mostly be limited to the US as well, this drastically opens up their reach in a few years from now. Assuming the partnership goes well, we see this is a likely stepping point for Affirm to start expanding internationally. We view this as a major win for Affirm and will allow them to start working with more brands overseas as they continue to build up credibility with other businesses and consumers!

 

Affirm Wrap-up:

While the market has appreciated the partnership with Amazon as well, we think they are missing the mark on the long term implications for the company that we discussed above.

These long term implications have us increasing our revenue targets for Affirm drastically in the years 2022 and beyond. While their eventual global expansion will hurt margins, we are okay accepting this as they need to grow as fast and possible now and can worry about optimizing their business in the long term. Affirm's path to victory will come more via partnerships with major retailers than due to any "advanced technologies". Therefore we can be comfortable with their strategy of expanding at all costs in order for them to grab market share quickly. While it is not a winner take all market, relationships will be key for them to continue to stay competitive over the longer term.

Therefore, our price target below mostly accounts for increased top-line growth, and long term partnerships with global companies. We're truly excited for the future of this company and think the partnership with Amazon was the first major stepping stone for them.

 

 

Price Target: $122 (33% upside)

Target Date: Q1 2022

Rating: Overweight

Ticker: AFRM
