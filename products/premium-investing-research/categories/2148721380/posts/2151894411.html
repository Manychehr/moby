Johnson & Johnson: Single Dose Vaccines?

Published on December 11, 2020

Stock: Johnson & Johnson (JNJ)

Rating: Overweight

Price Target: $175

Target Date: 10/2021

 

 

Headline:

While companies like Moderna, Pfizer and Astrazeneca have grabbed headlines over the last several months, Johnson & Johnson (JNJ) has also been working on another vaccine. Unlike the three other mentioned above, JNJ's vaccine would actually be a single dose! We believe, if approved, this single dose vaccine will be a key player in the distribution process! While the market has priced in a lot of general vaccine upside to this point, we believe investors have discounted JNJ's contribution to the overall solution. For it will not be a winner take all vaccine, but rather a blended mix of several options, in order to vaccinate the world as quickly as possible.

 

Details:

When looking closer at JNJ's vaccine solution, should it be approved - which we are anticipating, we believe JNJ is in a position to manufacture and distribute the vaccine at greater scale than any other provider. Pair this with the single dose usage, and we believe JNJ has the potential to scale their solution rapidly (once approved). Having said that JNJ's solution is based on an adenovirus platform - calling into question their efficacy.

When digging more into the efficacy of their platform, we have seen their Ad26 (adenovirus) platform be purposed for both Ebola, Zika and HIV! This demonstrates directly the potential of their vector in applying this to the novel coronavirus. However, what we've seen in previous trials is that efficacy could be the 70-90% range. While these numbers have not yet been finalized, we are aware that expectations should be less than the current 2 approved solutions. While not as effective, given the global shortage, we still see JNJ's solution being ordered and shipped at global scale.

When also looking more closely at the solution, we see a similar method being applied via the spike protein akin to Moderna & Pfizer. Therefore we believe the comparisons with Astrazeneca are unwarranted and therefore should be more effective (Astrazeneca around the 70% mark). We also believe this solution may be "safer" as we as more durable. This is what we are anticipating, but will monitor the results of their phase three trials.

Please note - We are looking at this from an investing standpoint. This is not healthcare advice. Please do not choose a vaccine, based off of this information. You should still listen to your public health officials.

When factoring in the implications of these projections, we see the vaccine being rolled out over a multi-year period. The first period would consist of global vaccinations at scale, looking to end the pandemic. The second period would be vaccines (treated as boosters) to newborns, and others looking to keep at bay any mutations in the virus and keeping immunity up. Therefore, we project JNJ's solution (if approved) to provide financial stability for years to come. When factoring this into our DCF model, we project out additional free cash flow with improved margins, therefore boosting our price target to $175. We therefore are overweight, and will continue to monitor their trials!
