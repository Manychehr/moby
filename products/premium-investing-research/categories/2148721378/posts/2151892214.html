General Motors (GM): Can they capture the EV market?

Published on June 17, 2021

We received a large amount of follow up regarding our post on Instagram yesterday discussing cars sales this summer and the future of EV's/autonomous cars! In the post itself we discussed GM, Tesla, Ford and some others but wanted to use this post to dive in deeper on GM specifically and tackle the question, "Can they capture the EV market"? Let's break it down further below:

If you've been reading our research for awhile we may sound like a broken record in regards to GM as we've been covering them quite often. If you have not, welcome to one of our most coveted names.

For the TLDR: Please see the price target at the bottom of the page.

Intro:

One of the reasons we love GM is because they "hoped" on the EV train well before many others and have successfully been making the jump from combustion based engines to electric ones for the last several years - well ahead of the curve. As many other companies have come to the realization that EV is here to stay, GM made that realization awhile ago and therefore positioned themselves as the next best alternative to Tesla in the immediate future. While Tesla has pure dominance of market share in the EV market, GM is gearing up to not only be #2 but eventually overtake them all! Let's discuss all the new details of what they're doing that is so exciting. If you want to read the details leading up to this point, please check out all of the posts we've covered historically on GM once signed in to the platform!

  1. GM is putting their money where their mouth is more-so than they ever have. While most companies to this point have committed capital to EV's, the investment relative to their balance sheet is extremely small. Comparing this to GM we see that they just increased their commitments to EV's by saying they will $35 billion by 2030 (75% higher than the pre-covid estimates). Looking at their balance sheet today (which will grow significantly by 2030) we see cash and short term investments at only $32 billion. The takeaway? GM has and continues to make the proper investments in the future of the company.
  2. While it is great that they're making the proper investments it doesn't mean much if they can't execute. And let us tell you GM is executing. As we mentioned in our post yesterday, GM's CEO said that they expect their results in the first half of 2021 to be significantly better than originally forecasted. Pair that with a massive forecasted increase in overall car sales with supply down and we're forecasting that GM could increase their earnings by over 20% (>1$) over the next months/quarters. We think this event could be the catalyst for another move upwards for the GM stock.
  3. However in the longer term another X-Factor people aren't watching for is when we see a new type of investor flood the market - the ESG investor. This is because to date, it it has been extremely hard to make a realistic buy case for the ESG investor given GM's current corporate strategy (more sales coming from dirty energy than clean. However once GM makes the flip, an entirely new funnel of cash will come flooding this stock. Once GM starts seeing more sales from EV's, we believe these types of investors will start to be able to make the case. With billions of potential dollars able to flood the stock, we think this could add serious demand never-seen before for GM. If this framework materializes quickly we think this could even cause the stock to spike past our $8o price target up to our $100 bull case!

 

Wrap-up:

Summarizing this up, not only is GM making the proper investments in the EV industry, they're capitalizing on it now. While it is yet to be seen who will be the true winner/winners, we can confidently say GM will be in that group in some capacity. Should they get there soon they have a secret weapon in terms of the ESG investor sitting in the fold. With short term sales spiking, EV investments doing well and ESG investors potentially coming soon, we see GM as a great investment as part of the play into electrification of vehicles!
