2 Major Awards That Will Help This Defense Contractor Grow!

 
 

Published on September 16, 2021 

Last we wrote about Raytheon Technologies there was one major factor that made them extremely attractive.

And that is the United States Government.

Why? It is because Raytheon pulls is ~60% of their revenue from the sale of defense products and services. The United States alone accounts for 61% of these sales. The reason the US government accounts for such a large portion of their revenue is because the US spends more money on military defense than any other country in the world! With this unlikely to change anytime soon, Raytheon is in a position to constantly be pulling in revenue. However, Raytheon is no one trick pony. They also have a massive presence in the commercial airlines world that has nice tailwinds coming into play for the stock. We therefore are adjusting our price target upwards on the news, and are excited for what is next. Let's jump into the details and the projected price below!

 

Raytheon Overview:

If you're unfamiliar with Raytheon, they are the product of the combination of the two giants: United Technology Corporation & Raytheon Technologies Corporation. This massive company is then broken up into four subsidiaries: Collins Aerospace Systems, Pratt & Whitney, Raytheon Intelligence & Space and Raytheon Missiles & Defense. The core of our focus today will be on two of these four business lines. They are:

  • Pratt & Whitney: Pratt & Whitney is the leading supplier of aircraft engines for commercial, military, business and general aviation customers. It also produces turbo engines.
  • Raytheon Missiles & Defense: Raytheon Missiles & Defense provides a broad range of technologies related to air and missile defense systems, radars, precision systems and control systems to 30 countries.

While we wrote about the extraordinary reach of these two business lines last time theres some new developments we want to double click into.

 

Pratt & Whitney: 

As we mentioned above their subsidiary, Pratt & Whitney, heavily plays into consumer airspace. What we see across the industry therefore is two strong signals:

  • The first is that air traffic has stayed strong this year with mileage traveled increasing as the months go on. This is one of the most important metrics to track and is highly encouraging.
  • The second is that while revenue per passenger miles has slowed down (popular metric to track to in this sector) due to concerns around the Delta variant, RTX said they're seeing positive trends starting to turn back the other way. This is something that we're actively monitoring should it change the other way. But in any case, we see this trend reversing over the long term and are not concerned about any short term headwinds here.

The next section is what is truly promising though.

 

Raytheon Missiles & Defense:

While the commercial business above is recovering post-COVID, the real strength in this company comes from this business unit. What recently happened was that RTX won a number of key awards last quarter, including the Long-Range Standoff Weapon System (~$2B) and Next Generation Interceptor ($1.3B). While the Biden administration will spend less on defense over the next few years than the Trump administration did, this awards are huge for Raytheon. This will translate into massive growth opportunities in revenue as their defense systems are clearly superior to their competition. While we like the company before, this alone, warrants the strength of our thesis in them.

 

Wrap-up:

As we've been mentioning all year, having a portion of your portfolio dedicated to companies like this is paramount. In an environment where super growth stocks valuations are getting hit, strong solid companies like Raytheon can consistently grow in the background. While they will never get you 100% YoY returns, the more important point for them is that their risk/reward skews so high. They're a dependable company that will appreciate over the next year, without taking on much risk.

Therefore we are adding to our existing position, and re-affirming our belief in the company. With the rebound in travel and the strength of their defense business, our projections have us increasing our price target for the company. We're excited to see what comes next!
